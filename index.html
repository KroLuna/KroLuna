What is Scrum

Scrum is a framework that helps people, teamworks and others to effektively find solutions to complex problems. Scrum was created by Ken Schwaber and Jeff Sutherland in the early 90's and it was presented in 1995. They have written the Scrum Guide to help people understand Scrum.

It is an empirical process, meaning that we adapt to our team so our team works as efficiently as it goes. We make changes to improve and the team is constantly learning and adapting.

There are three factors that are important in Scrum and those are:

    Transparency

    Adaptation

    Inspection

The Scrum values are:

    Courage

    Focus

    Commitment

    Respect

    Openness

There is always a Scrum Team with between 3 to 9 members, were one of them is the Scrum Master, the other is the Product Owner and then the developers.

To understand Scrum better, visit the Scrum Guide.
